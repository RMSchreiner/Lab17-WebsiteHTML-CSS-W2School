Neuro-toxic Sea Creatures

There are many different types of Cnidaria. This is the phylum of animal species that include jellyfish and sea anemone. Additionally this phylum includes different types of corals. A distinguishing characteristic of this phylum of sea creatures is the presence of cnidocytes. These cnidocytes are used primarly to capture prey. The cnidocyte acts like a harpoon shooting out from polyps on the side of corals. These tiny harpoons puncture flesh and inject venomous neuro-toxins into its prey.
